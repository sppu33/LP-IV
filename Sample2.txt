Qutub Minar is one of the most prominent monuments in India, located in Delhi. It was built by Qutb-ud-din Aibak in 1193 to celebrate the victory of Muslim rule in India. Made of red and buff sandstone, it stands tall at more than seventy meters, making it one of the tallest brick minarets in the world. The tower is surrounded by ancient ruins and beautifully carved structures, reflecting the blend of Indo-Islamic architecture. Qutub Minar has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and continues to attract tourists from around the globe for its historical and architectural importance.
